What is Scout Inbound?

Scout Inbound is your personal dashboard for tracking job applications as they arrive. Every time a candidate applies to one of your jobs through the Scout Genius job board, they appear here automatically — no manual entry needed. The system handles importing them into Bullhorn and keeps you updated on what's happening with each application in real time.

Your Dashboard at a Glance

At the top of your dashboard you'll see four summary cards. These give you a quick snapshot of how your applications are flowing:

Total Applicants
Everyone who has applied to your jobs so far — across all time.
Processed
Applications that were successfully added to Bullhorn with no issues.
Failed
A small number that ran into a technical hiccup. Your admin is automatically notified when this happens.
Duplicate Rate
The percentage of applicants who have applied before. Helps you spot repeat candidates.

The Candidates List

Below the summary cards, you'll see a table listing every candidate who has applied to your jobs. Here's what each column means:

Column What it tells you
Name The candidate's name. Click the link icon beside it to open their record directly in Bullhorn.
Job Applied To The job they applied for. Click the link icon to open that job order in Bullhorn.
Source Where the candidate came from — for example, LinkedIn Job Board, Dice, Indeed, or a direct application through the website.
Status What's currently happening with their application (see below).
Date Applied When they submitted their application.

Understanding Application Status

The status badge next to each candidate tells you exactly where their application stands:

Status What it means
Completed The candidate was successfully added to Bullhorn. Their resume, contact details, and application are all there waiting for you.
Processing The system is currently importing this candidate. This usually takes less than a minute — refresh the page and it should update.
Failed Something went wrong during import. This is rare. Your system administrator has been automatically notified and will resolve it.

Duplicate Candidates

If a candidate has applied before — either to this job or another one — you'll see a Duplicate flag next to their name along with a confidence percentage. A higher percentage means the system is more certain this person is already in your Bullhorn database.

A duplicate flag is just a heads-up — it doesn't block the application or remove the candidate. It's there so you can review and decide how to handle it yourself, rather than the system making that call for you.

Opening Records in Bullhorn

Next to candidate names and job titles, you'll notice small link icons (). Clicking one opens that candidate or job record directly in Bullhorn in a new tab — so you can jump straight from your dashboard into your ATS without any searching.

Does It Refresh Automatically?

Yes — the dashboard refreshes itself every 60 seconds automatically. You don't need to hit the browser refresh button or reload the page. New applicants will appear on their own as they come in.

Tip: If you want to see the very latest results immediately, you can still refresh your browser manually at any time. The auto-refresh just means you never have to.

Who Can See What?

Scout Inbound only shows jobs that are assigned to you in Bullhorn. If you're an account manager covering three open roles, you'll see only those three jobs and the candidates who applied to them. Other recruiters' jobs and applicants are not visible to you, and yours are not visible to them.
