What is Scout Screening?

Scout Screening reads every resume that comes in for your jobs and gives it an AI match score before you ever have to look at it. Think of it as a tireless first-pass reviewer that works around the clock — so by the time you open your portal, the candidates most likely to be a good fit are already at the top, clearly labelled, with notes explaining why.

Your Dashboard at a Glance

At the top of the Screening Portal you'll see four clickable summary cards:

Qualified Matches
Candidates whose AI score meets or exceeds your threshold. These are worth a closer look.
Pending Review
Candidates currently being analyzed by the AI. They'll appear in the list within minutes.
Not Recommended
Candidates who fell below the threshold. Still accessible, but the AI flagged significant gaps.
Screened This Week
Total candidates the AI has reviewed across all your jobs in the last 7 days.
Tip: Click any card to instantly filter the candidates list below to show only that group. Click again to clear the filter.

Understanding Match Scores

Every candidate receives a score from 0 to 100. The higher the score, the closer their resume matches what the job is asking for. Here's a simple guide to what the ranges mean:

80 and above
Strong match — meets most key requirements
60 to 79
Partial match — some gaps worth reviewing
Below 60
Significant gaps — likely not the right fit

These scores are guides, not final decisions. A candidate at 78 might still be excellent — use the AI notes alongside the score to form your own view.

Qualified vs. Not Recommended

Each candidate in the list carries one of two badges:

Badge What it means What to do
Qualified Score is at or above your threshold for this job. Review the AI notes, then open the candidate in Bullhorn to decide next steps.
Not Recommended Score fell below the threshold — the AI identified notable gaps. You can still review the notes to understand why, and open the record in Bullhorn if you disagree with the AI.
Remember: The AI's assessment is a starting point. You always have the final say on any candidate. If you think the AI got it wrong, trust your instincts — you know your clients best.

Reading the AI Notes

Next to each candidate, you'll see a short snippet of what the AI found. To see the full picture, click "View full notes" — this opens a panel with the complete AI assessment, which typically includes:

  • 1 A summary of which skills and experience matched the job well.
  • 2 Any gaps or missing requirements the AI identified.
  • 3 The reasoning behind the score — written in plain English, not code or jargon.
  • 4 A direct link to open the candidate's full profile in Bullhorn.

Setting Your Match Threshold

The threshold is the minimum score a candidate needs to show up as Qualified for a specific job. The default is 80. You can change it per job directly in the portal.

In the job settings table at the bottom of the page, find the job you want to adjust. Click into the number in the Threshold column, type a new value between 50 and 100, and it saves automatically — no buttons to click.

When to lower the threshold: If a role is hard to fill and you're seeing very few Qualified results, try lowering to 70 or 65.

When to raise the threshold: If a senior or highly specialized role is getting too many Qualified results and you want to be more selective, try raising to 85 or 90.

Custom Requirements — Steering the AI

By default, the AI reads the job description in Bullhorn and figures out what to look for. But sometimes you know there's a specific tool, certification, or skill that's non-negotiable — something that might not be clearly stated in the job description.

Click the "Configure" button next to any job to open the requirements panel. You'll see what the AI is currently using (read-only), and a text box where you can add your own instructions. For example: "Must have direct experience with SAP S/4HANA — general SAP experience is not sufficient."

Once saved, the AI will factor in your instruction for all future candidates screened against that job. Existing results are not retroactively rescored.

Opening Records in Bullhorn

Candidate names and job titles in the table are linked. Clicking the link icon beside them opens the corresponding record in Bullhorn in a new tab. You can review the full resume, add notes, or take any next step — all without losing your place in the Screening Portal.

Auto-Refresh

The portal refreshes automatically every 60 seconds, so new screening results appear on their own as candidates come in. You'll see the table update without needing to reload the page.

Who Can See What?

The Screening Portal only shows results for jobs assigned to you in Bullhorn. Scores and AI notes for another recruiter's jobs are not visible in your view, and yours are not visible to them. Each recruiter sees only their own workload.
