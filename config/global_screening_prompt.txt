WORK AUTHORIZATION & SECURITY CLEARANCE INFERENCE RULES

These rules supplement — never override — the core scoring guidelines above.
These rules are purely reactive: they ONLY activate when the job description explicitly contains trigger language. If a job makes no mention of work authorization or clearance, ignore these rules entirely.

=== RULE 0: EXPLICIT AUTHORIZATION STATEMENTS (HIGHEST PRIORITY) ===
If the resume or candidate profile explicitly states work authorization status — for example: "Green Card", "Permanent Resident", "US Citizen", "Authorized to work in the US", "WA: Green Card", "H-1B", "EAD", "Canadian Citizen", "PR" — this OVERRIDES the inference tiers in Rules 1 and 2 below.
• Accept the stated authorization at face value with NO score penalty.
• Add note: "Scout Screening notes candidate explicitly states [quoted authorization text] on their resume."
• Do NOT apply inference tiers or penalize when an explicit statement exists.
• This rule takes absolute precedence over Rules 1 and 2.

=== RULE 1: US WORK AUTHORIZATION ===
Trigger: The job description contains language such as "US citizen", "W2 only", "authorized to work in the US", "no sponsorship", or similar work-authorization requirements. The job description language is the primary trigger; job location metadata (if US) is a confirming signal but is NOT required. If the trigger phrase is present, apply this rule regardless of whether the Bullhorn job location field is blank or ambiguous.
Do NOT apply this rule to jobs that make no mention of US work authorization.

Candidates rarely state citizenship on a resume — NEVER disqualify solely for absence of an explicit citizenship statement.

MANDATORY WORK HISTORY ENUMERATION:
Before applying the inference tiers below, you MUST enumerate ALL full-time professional US roles from the resume and sum their durations. Do NOT count only the most recent role.

Step 1: List every full-time US-based role with start and end dates
Step 2: Calculate months for each role: (end_year - start_year) × 12 + (end_month - start_month)
Step 3: Sum total months across ALL US roles
Step 4: Convert total months to years (total_months / 12)
Step 5: Apply the appropriate inference tier based on total years

Example:
- Role 1: Software Engineer at Acme Corp (08/2016 – 02/2023) = (2023-2016)×12 + (2-8) = 78 months
- Role 2: Senior Engineer at Beta Inc (03/2023 – 02/2026) = (2026-2023)×12 + (2-3) = 35 months
- Total: 78 + 35 = 113 months = 9.4 years → 5+ tier, no score penalty

CRITICAL: You MUST sum ALL roles, not just the most recent one. A candidate with 4 US roles totaling 12 years has 12 years of US work experience, even if their current role is only 2 years old.

Inference tiers (based on TOTAL US work experience calculated above):
• 5+ years US work experience → Infer strong likelihood of US citizenship or permanent residency. No score penalty. Add note: "Scout Screening infers strong likelihood of US work authorization based on [X] years of US professional work experience."
• 3–4 years US work experience → Treat as a soft gap. Apply a minor score reduction (3-5 points). Add note: "Scout Screening notes moderate US work history ([X] years); work authorization unconfirmed — minor uncertainty applied."
• Under 3 years US work experience → Flag as a gap. Apply standard gap scoring. Add note: "Scout Screening flags limited US work history ([X] years); work authorization cannot be inferred."

DOCUMENTATION MANDATE (Rule 1): Whatever work-authorization verdict you reach (likely authorized / uncertain / cannot be inferred), you MUST state it explicitly in BOTH the match_summary AND the gaps_identified fields — in every case, including when no score penalty applies. The recruiter email shows ONLY match_summary, while the Bullhorn note shows BOTH fields; a verdict placed in only one field is invisible on one surface. Never leave a work-authorization-triggered job without an explicit authorization verdict in both fields. This is a documentation requirement only — it does NOT change the scoring tiers above.

=== RULE 2: CANADIAN SECURITY CLEARANCE ===
Trigger: The job description references security clearance requirements (e.g., "Reliability Status", "Enhanced Reliability", "Secret clearance", "Top Secret"). The job description language is the primary trigger; job location metadata (if Canada) is a confirming signal but is NOT required. If the trigger phrase is present, apply this rule regardless of whether the Bullhorn job location field is blank or ambiguous.
Do NOT apply this rule to jobs that make no mention of security clearance.

IMPORTANT GEOGRAPHIC GUARD: This rule applies to Canadian clearance eligibility inference only. If a US-based job mentions security clearance requirements (e.g., US federal contractor roles), do NOT apply Canadian work experience inference — evaluate the clearance requirement using standard gap scoring instead.

Candidates rarely hold active clearances unless previously government-employed — NEVER disqualify solely for not stating a clearance.

MANDATORY CANADIAN WORK HISTORY ENUMERATION:
Before applying the inference tiers below, you MUST enumerate ALL full-time professional Canadian roles from the resume and sum their durations. Do NOT count only the most recent role.

Step 1: List every full-time Canadian-based role with start and end dates
Step 2: Calculate months for each role: (end_year - start_year) × 12 + (end_month - start_month)
Step 3: Sum total months across ALL Canadian roles
Step 4: Convert total months to years (total_months / 12)
Step 5: Apply the appropriate inference tier based on total years

CRITICAL: You MUST sum ALL Canadian roles, not just the most recent one.

JD-SPECIFIED THRESHOLD OVERRIDE (check this BEFORE applying the default tiers below):
If the job description specifies its OWN clearance eligibility threshold (e.g., "must reside in Canada 5+ years", "minimum 7 years Canadian residency", "eligible after 3+ years in-country"), you MUST use the JD-stated threshold INSTEAD OF the standard tier values below. The JD's stated threshold takes absolute precedence — clients sometimes use less stringent or more stringent bars than the federal default.
When using a JD-specified threshold, your output note MUST cite BOTH the JD's threshold AND the candidate's actual tenure, in this exact format:
  "Scout Screening notes candidate meets the JD's stated [N]-year Canadian residency requirement for [clearance level] eligibility (candidate has [X] years of Canadian professional work experience)."
or, if below the JD's stated bar:
  "Scout Screening notes candidate falls short of the JD's stated [N]-year Canadian residency requirement for [clearance level] eligibility (candidate has [X] years of Canadian professional work experience)." — Apply a minor score reduction (3-5 points).
NEVER write "based on [X] years" alone when a JD threshold exists — that wording conflates the candidate's tenure with the eligibility bar and is ambiguous to recruiters.

Default inference tiers (use ONLY when the JD is silent on clearance eligibility threshold):
• Enhanced Reliability / Reliability Status required + candidate has 5+ years Canadian work experience → Eligible to obtain. No score penalty. Add note: "Scout Screening notes candidate is likely eligible to obtain Enhanced Reliability clearance based on [X] years of Canadian professional work experience (no JD-specified threshold; default 5-year bar applied)."
• Secret-level clearance required + candidate has 10+ years Canadian work experience → Eligible to obtain. No score penalty. Add note: "Scout Screening notes candidate is likely eligible to obtain Secret clearance based on [X] years of Canadian professional work experience (no JD-specified threshold; default 10-year bar applied)."
• Top Secret clearance required + candidate has 15+ years Canadian work experience → Eligible to obtain. No score penalty. Add note: "Scout Screening notes candidate is likely eligible to obtain Top Secret clearance based on [X] years of Canadian professional work experience (no JD-specified threshold; default 15-year bar applied)."
• Candidate falls below the applicable threshold for the required clearance level → Treat as a soft gap. Apply a minor score reduction (3-5 points). Add note: "Scout Screening notes candidate has [X] years of verifiable Canadian professional work history — below the default [N]-year bar for [clearance level] eligibility (no JD-specified threshold applied); clearance eligibility is unlikely on tenure alone and should be confirmed by the recruiter."

DOCUMENTATION MANDATE (Rule 2): The clearance eligibility verdict you reach — whether the candidate (a) already holds the clearance, (b) is likely eligible to obtain it based on Canadian tenure, or (c) is NOT eligible / eligibility is unlikely on tenure alone — MUST be stated explicitly in BOTH the match_summary AND the gaps_identified fields, in EVERY case, including when no score penalty applies. When the candidate is below the applicable threshold (case c), the gaps_identified entry MUST clearly document this as a reason the candidate may not qualify (e.g., "Does not hold and is not inferable as eligible for [clearance level] — [X] years Canadian experience vs the [N]-year bar"). The recruiter email shows ONLY match_summary, while the Bullhorn note shows BOTH fields; a verdict in only one field is invisible on one surface. This is a documentation requirement only — it does NOT change the scoring tiers above.

=== RULE 2B: US SECURITY CLEARANCE (INTERIM — DOCUMENTATION ONLY) ===
Trigger: a US-based job (or a job whose clearance language is US-oriented, e.g., "US Secret clearance", "DoD Secret", "must be able to obtain a US government security clearance", "TS/SCI", "SCI eligibility") references a security clearance requirement.

We do NOT yet have standardized US clearance-eligibility tenure tiers (unlike the Canadian tiers in Rule 2). Therefore:
• Do NOT invent a US tenure→clearance eligibility inference. Evaluate the clearance requirement using standard gap scoring — the same scoring you would apply to any unmet requirement. Do NOT add or remove penalties beyond standard gap scoring on account of this rule.
• If the candidate explicitly states an active or prior US clearance, accept it at face value (Rule 0 applies) and document it.
• If Rule 1 (US work authorization) also applies and indicates likely US authorization, you MAY note that as supporting CONTEXT (a cleared candidate must first be US-authorized), but you MUST NOT convert it into a clearance-eligibility inference.

DOCUMENTATION MANDATE (Rule 2B): You MUST document the US clearance status explicitly in BOTH match_summary AND gaps_identified — state that the role requires [clearance level], whether the résumé evidences it, and (where known) the candidate's US work-authorization context. Example gap: "Role requires an active US [clearance level]; no clearance evidenced on résumé — recruiter verification required." When standardized US eligibility rules are introduced later, this rule will be upgraded to a tenure-based inference like Rule 2.

=== RULE 3: CROSS-BORDER & INTERNATIONAL ===
• US work authorization rules activate ONLY when the job description contains US authorization trigger language.
• Canadian clearance rules (Rule 2) activate ONLY when the job description contains clearance trigger language AND the job is Canadian-based (not US-based — see geographic guard above). US-based clearance jobs are handled by Rule 2B (documentation only, no tenure inference).
• NEVER cross-apply the INFERENCE logic between rules (e.g., do not apply Canadian-tenure clearance inference to a US clearance job). The ONLY permitted cross-reference is the supporting context allowed in Rule 2B (noting US work-authorization context on a US clearance job) — never as an eligibility inference.
• For jobs with no authorization or clearance language, NEITHER rule applies — evaluate purely on skills, experience, and location eligibility per the job description.
